Supply Chain Tracking FAQ

Everything you need to know about blockchain-verified product authenticity

The Basics

What is supply chain tracking and why does it matter?

Supply chain tracking monitors products as they move from manufacturer → distributor → retailer → customer. It matters because:

  • Consumer demand: 73% of consumers say they'd pay more for products with complete transparency
  • Counterfeit prevention: $4.2 trillion in counterfeit goods sold globally each year
  • Regulatory compliance: Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA) requires traceability
  • Brand protection: One fake product can destroy years of brand trust
  • ESG/Sustainability: Companies must prove ethical sourcing claims

What is BizTrack?

BizTrack is blockchain-powered supply chain tracking made affordable for small and medium businesses. We let you:

  • Mint product records to the XRP Ledger (immutable, tamper-proof)
  • Store unlimited product data on IPFS (decentralized storage)
  • Generate QR codes for instant customer verification
  • Prove authenticity without expensive enterprise software

How It Works

How does blockchain make this better than a regular database?

Regular databases can be edited. A company could mint a "2025 vintage" wine in 2026 and backdate it. Trust relies on trusting the company.

Blockchain is immutable. Once written to the XRP Ledger, it cannot be changed. Trust is built into the technology, not the company. The blockchain is public—anyone can verify without asking us.

Isn't blockchain expensive and slow?

Bitcoin/Ethereum: Yes, expensive ($5-50 per transaction) and slow (10+ minutes).

XRP Ledger (what we use): No. Transactions cost $0.00003 and settle in 3-5 seconds. We're using the right tool for the job.

Do my customers need to understand blockchain?

No! They scan a QR code and see product info. "Blockchain" is backend infrastructure—customers just see "Verified ✓"

It's like HTTPS encryption: users don't understand SSL certificates, they just see the lock icon and trust the site.

What if I want to update product information later?

You mint a new record with updated info. The blockchain history shows: original mint → update. Transparency means showing changes, not hiding them.

How BizTrack Compares

How are companies currently doing supply chain tracking?

1. Paper-based systems: Certificates, invoices. Problem: Easy to forge, data gets lost.

2. Centralized databases (SAP, Oracle): Cost $50K-$500K/year, requires IT team, one company controls all data.

3. QR codes → databases: Company can change the data after the fact, no proof of authenticity.

4. RFID tags: Expensive hardware ($1,000s), tracks location but doesn't prove authenticity.

5. Enterprise blockchain (IBM Food Trust, VeChain): Built for Fortune 500, costs $500K+, requires blockchain expertise.
